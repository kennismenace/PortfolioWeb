SENTENCES OF RESTRICTION #11


John Baldessari's improvised melodies of So LeWitt's 35 Sentences on Conceptual Art. Baldessari's intention was to expose LeWitt's text to a larger public of what art could be and what artist could do. The given restrictions for this project were to be purely typographic, purely self-made imagery-based, and only one sentence from Sentences on Conceptual Art. This poster is sentence #11:


"Ideas do not necessarily proceed in logical order. They may set one off in unexpected directions, but an idea must necessarily be completed in the mind before the next one is formed."



Purpose - Content & Visual Study, Experiment
What is it - Typography, Illustration
Instructor - Scott Thorpe
