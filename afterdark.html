AFTER DARK


After Dark is an exhibition that only exist as both a single page microsite and a printable poster series. Creating a transmedia work that can exist in both digital and print. This exhibition is about the anonymity and ephemerality of the screensaver that were popular in the 90â€™s.

A screensaver is a computer program that blanks the screen or fills it with moving images or patterns when the computer is not in use. Back in the day the original purpose of screensavers were to prevent phosphor burn-in on CRT and plasma computer monitors. Screensavers were pieces of moving art with no information of who designed them, but this microsite exhibits just that: what, when, and who.

PROCESS:

1. Research on browser characteristics.
2. Study works that inhabits the web space.
3. Note and collect artefacts.


Purpose - Exploration, Speculative, Experiment
What is it - HTML, CSS, JS
Instructor - Jon Sueda, Catherine Schmidt
